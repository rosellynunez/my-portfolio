Festina and Lotus Connected D Landing Pages Design

Festina Group is a leading name in the watchmaking world with a long history and a global reach spanning over 90 countries. This project is due to Festina Group wanted to introduce two New Connected Watch Models: the Festina Connected D and the Lotus Hybrid Connected.

About

Both the Festina Connected D and the Lotus Connected D belong to their respective brand's connected watch collections, each with its unique style and audience appeal. The Festina Connected D is part of a more classic and premium collection, while the Lotus Hybrid Connected targets a younger demographic with a more modern design. This landing pages were integral to the introduction to these two new collections.

How I helped

I was tasked with creating landing pages for two distinct brands under the Festina Group umbrella: Festina and Lotus. These two brands have completely different styles and target audiences, but both required cohesive yet unique digital experiences that reflected their individual identities. Despite the differences in branding, the landing pages needed to follow a unified structure. This decision helped us meet the deadline while ensuring that each page respected the distinct branding.
Working with the Spain-based marketing team and the strategy team in Sweden, I was responsible for the UI design and also collaborated closely with the development team, which maintained the pages using Bootstrap.

1. Strategic Design for Connected D

To design the Festina Connected D page, the focus was on aligning Festina's core values—Quality, Tradition, and Technology—with the product's modern features. The goal was to balance timeless elegance with smart functionality, crafting a user-friendly design that highlights the watch's classic appeal and tech innovations. This involved creating a structure that could also showcase the benefits of the Lotus Connected D, ensuring a consistent visual and communication guideline across both products. Despite time constraints, we successfully emphasized the watches as smarter tools for daily life, showcasing features like health monitoring, exercise tracking, and ease of use, while maintaining Festina's hallmark elegance.

For the Lotus Connected D page, the concept revolved around modernity and elegance—key values deeply tied to the Lotus brand. In addition, it emphasized cutting-edge technology, seamlessly integrated to enhance the user's daily life. The watch combines advanced hybrid features, such as smart notifications and activity tracking, with a sleek design, making it a versatile companion for various scenarios, from professional settings to sports activities. 2. Cross-Team Collaboration for Seamless Execution

Collaboration with marketing, strategic and development areas was essential to bring this vision to life. I worked closely with Spanish and Swedish teams to ensure alignment between design, marketing and business objectives. On the development side, I partnered with the team to make the design posible underline Bootstrap guides. This required close coordination to maintain visual consistency and ensure a seamless user experience on both desktop and mobile platforms. Also, I was i charged for curating and resizing the graphic materials for optimal performance across devices.

3. UI/UX Approach

From a UX standpoint, the main goal was to simplify navigation while guiding users through the product features. I employed clear visual hierarchy and CTA buttons that stand out, with sufficient white space to keep the design clean and focused. Buttons like “See collection” are designed to be visible and guide the user toward specific actions, in this case, was directig to the collection page.

4. Creating Versatile Components

I led the creation of the components that could be easily adapted across different pages for both brands and other websites in the company's portfolio.
I used Figma to design the components, incorporating design tokens for consistency, and regularly consulted with the development area to ensure that everything was technically feasible. To streamline the design-to-development process, I also utilized Figma plugins to ensure an efficient handoff, and we integrated Storybook to manage and test components.

4.1 fgp-ui-item-card Component

  • Purpose: Provide a compact structure to highlight elements such as images, titles, descriptions and action buttons.
    Facilitate user navigation by highlighting features of the product.
  • Use: Desktop: I used images with a standard size of 2560px, optimized for large and Retina displays. And they were resized according to number of columns. Mobile (991px): The 991px breakpoint (according Bootstrap's grid system) was used to ensure cards were responsive and easy to interact with on medium/small screens.
  • Example on the Landing Page: Each fgp-ui-item-card contains an image of the product, accompanied by a brief description and a button that redirects the user to more information (product page) or an action (check-out page).

4.2 fgp-ui-text-on-background Component

  • Purpose: It allows to highlight text over images.
  • Use: It will be find in areas where texts over hero images or sections with backgrounds. For example, the description of the hybrid watch or highlighted benefits could use this component to ensure clear reading regardless of the background color.
  • Personalization: It allows adjustments in the position, alignment and size of the text, making it versatile in responsive design.

5. Post-Development: QA and Design Refinements

After the development team implemented my designs, I conducted multiple rounds of QA (quality assurance) to ensure that everything—from paddings to design details—was pixel-perfect. I worked closely with the developers to adjust small design elements, ensuring that both the user experience and the overall look and feel of the landing pages met the high standards we had set. We made sure that the transition from Figma to the live website was smooth, tweaking any issues that arose during the development phase.

6. Final Results

We successfully launched this two landing pages that reflected the individual characteristics of the Festina and Lotus brands. The collaborative effort between the UI design, development, and marketing teams resulted in landing pages that were visually compelling and effective at communicating the features and appeal of the new watch collections.
