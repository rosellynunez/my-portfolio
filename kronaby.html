Kronaby Web Page Redesing

About

Kronaby designs hybrid watches that blend cutting-edge technology with minimalist Swedish design.

Low direct traffic and sales hindered their growth. The PrestaShop platform created inefficiencies in design, development, and marketing processes.

Our solution: a complete website redesign and migration to Festina's unified, Bootstrap-based digital ecosystem. This created a cohesive brand experience and streamlined design and development workflows.

See it alive

How I helped

I was responsible for the entire UX/UI process for the Kronaby website redesign. I started by the research and competitive analysis. Then, I developed a design that truly reflected the Kronaby brand. From there, it was all about wireframing, prototyping, and collaborating closely with the strategy, marketing, and development teams to make it happen.

1. UX Decisions at Kronaby

Kicking off this project, I knew the Kronaby website needed a refresh and a strategic overhaul to drive business results. It also had to fit within Festina's existing digital ecosystem (which meant some design constraints, especially for product grids and pages). My focus was on improving what needed it while keeping what worked. Since the old site lacked any tracking, I started by:


  • Talking to users, stakeholders, and the Kronaby team to understand what was most important for both users and the business.

  • Checking out competitor sites to get ideas and optimize our e-commerce strategy for engagement and conversions.

  • The site's old content structure was a mess, which I simplified for a much better user experience and a stronger sales funnel. Also, the site had a crazy number of heading and paragraph styles which I streamlined for a cleaner and more readable experience.

4. Structure

I used low-fidelity wireframes to define the user flows, focusing on explaining Kronaby's hybrid watches and showcasing their features. These wireframes covered the homepage and landing pages because of the product and the carousel pages had to follow the Festina webpage. Once these were validated, I proceeded with high-fidelity designs.

5. UI Decisions: Colors, Typography, and Aesthetics

The Kronaby app used Avenir Next and Minion for its typography, but these fonts weren't available through Google Fonts and caused performance issues. My solution was DM Sans, a clean and minimalist font that reflects Kronaby's style and maintains readability.

The color scheme was crafted to reflect Kronaby's premium and modern identity. Neutral tones were paired with accent colors that highlighted the brand's innovative character.

Kronaby's color palette is built around a sophisticated mint green (#417178) and a complementary brown (#785954). A secondary palette, derived from the primary colors by adjusting their saturation and luminosity, was developed to provide visual interest and variety.
In addition to these, semantic colors played an important role.

The grill page uses its primary color mint-cool-100 on the back layer, and the primary mint-cool-900 for typography and iconography. A secondary color brown-vivid-500 is applied as an accent to the like iteractive icon.

6. Collaboration with Teams

I collaborated with the marketing manager in Spain and Swedish strategic team (including the Kronaby app team) to bring the brand to life online. Bi-weekly meetings kept us aligned and the project moving forward.

7. Adherence to Bootstrap Guidelines and components

The Kronaby redesign used Bootstrap for consistency and future compatibility with Festina's planned Tailwind CSS migration.
Component reuse was a priority, creating new ones only when essential and designing them to be scalable across other Festina projects. To clearly explain Kronaby's hybrid watch features, I designed a new component that I believe will be a valuable asset for other Festina brands as they add more features to their products.

9. What about metrics?

I finished this project in my last week at Festina Group, so I didn't have time to collect data. If I had stayed, I would have looked at

  • Direct Traffic (how many people visited the site directly)
  • Conversion Rate (how many people bought something)
  • Average Session Duration (how long people stayed on the site)
  • Bounce Rate (how quickly people left the site)

I redesigned the site to get more direct visitors and boost sales. I also wanted people to enjoy the site more, so they'd stay longer and wouldn't leave so fast. I simplified the navigation and made the important features stand out. A planned checkout redesign will further optimize the user experience.
