Kronaby Website Refresh

Background

Kronaby, a Swedish brand known for its technologically advanced and minimalist hybrid watches, required a significant digital update to better align with Festina's overall brand strategy. The existing PrestaShop platform presented notable design and development inefficiencies, which were impeding the brand's growth potential.

My Role & Contributions

As the UX/UI designer, I led the redesign of Kronaby's website, handling:

  • User research and competitive analysis to identify key issues.
  • Wireframing and prototyping in Figma.
  • Collaboration with strategy, marketing, and development teams to ensure smooth implementation.

1. Project Scope & Constraints

To address these challenges and ensure a successful integration within Festina's digital ecosystem, we adopted a phased approach. The project's scope was defined by the limitations of Festina's existing platform.

  • We prioritized the redesign of elements that could be modified independently, focusing on the homepage, landing pages, and other user-facing sections that were underperforming.
  • Sections deeply integrated with Festina's core systems, such as product pages and the checkout process, were reserved for a subsequent phase. This approach allowed us to implement swift improvements while minimizing disruption to Festina's overall online presence.

2. Hypothesis & Research Goals

To understand the initial issues, I checked it out myself, saw what competitors were doing, talked to people inside the company, chatted with potential users and my coworkers, and read through reviews online.

Hypothesis

From this initial analysis, I formulated the following hypothesis:

We believe that navigation on the Kronaby site is confusing, that users do not fully understand the concept of hybrid watches, and that the website has low visibility in search engines.

By improving site structure, refining content, and optimizing SEO, we can create a better user experience, increase engagement, and drive more conversions.

Research Goals

The research focused on three key areas designed to validate a hypothesis about usability and conversion issues.

üéØ
  • Enhacing site navigation and usability - I need to assess how users currently interact with the site and identify pain points in the structure.
  • Improving product messaging so users clearly understand hybrid watches - I need to understand users' expectations and how they perceive hybrid watches.
  • Strengthening SEO strategies to increase organic traffic - I need to analyze search performance and identify opportunities for optimization.

5. UI Decisions: Typography, colors and aesthetics

Typography: Balancing aesthetics and performance

Kronaby's style guide specified Avenir Next and Minion fonts, But the website was a mess! They were using Avenir Next, Lucida Grande, Iconochive, and sometimes even Roboto when the others weren't working. This inconsistency also impacted performance, as some of these fonts were not available on Google Fonts. So, I fixed it by switching to DM Sans. It's clean, kinda like Avenir Next, and it made the site look consistent and load faster.

Before

This image showcases the inconsistent and chaotic typography found on the original Kronaby website. Notice the jarring mix of Avenir Next, Lucida Grande, Iconochive, and even Roboto used throughout the site. This not only disrupted the visual flow but also hindered the website's performance.

Color Palette

Kronaby's brand identity is rooted in elegance and innovation, which I translated into a refined color scheme:

  • Primary Colors: A mint green (#417178), representing precision and technology, paired with a complementary brown (#785954) that adds warmth.
  • Secondary Palette: Developed by adjusting the saturation and luminosity of the primary colors, creating depth and visual variety.
  • Semantic Colors: Used strategically to enhance usability and reinforce key actions.

Application of colors

On the product grid page, I applied the brand colors to enhance clarity and engagement:

  • Background: Mint-cool-100 provides a subtle, elegant foundation.
  • Typography & Icons: Mint-cool-900, ensuring strong contrast and readability.
  • Interactive Elements: Brown-vivid-500 as an accent for interactive icons like the ‚Äúlike‚Äù button, creating a visually cohesive experience.

9. What about metrics?

I finished this project in my last week at Festina Group, so I didn't have time to track final metrics. However, if I had stayed, I would have measured:

  • Average Session Duration ‚Üí How long users stayed on the site.
  • Conversion Rate ‚Üí Percentage of visitors who initiated the checkout process. While ideally I'd track completed purchases, the checkout's UX remained largely unchanged, limiting the potential impact of the UI refresh on final sales.
  • Bounce Rate ‚Üí How quickly users left the site.
  • Direct Traffic ‚Üí How many users visited the site directly.

10. Next steps for Kronaby

Honestly, the big thing would be tracking user behavior and refine the site. More engaging content, like videos and a blog, would boost SEO and explain the product. Building a community with reviews and forums would be great. And of course, tying it all in better with the rest of Festina's world, especially that checkout redesign, would make the whole experience smoother.

11. Key takeaways & learnings

  • Getting insights without analytics: Even without analytics, I got valuable insights from qualitative methods. This showed me that research doesn't always need big data, and I can do something that works with what I have on hands.
  • Being flexible with research: I had to do some last-minute user testing right before development started. It wasn't ideal, but it was essential. This showed me that even under tight deadlines, finding ways to include user feedback is better than nothing.
  • Working around technical limits: Festina's existing digital setup required creative problem-solving and a lot of communnication. Collaborating with developers helped me find solutions that balanced design and technical limitations.
  • Focusing on what matters most: Instead of trying to fix everything, I priotized areas that were feasible in that moment.
  • SEO is Non-Negotiable: Even without analytics, it was clear SEO plays a huge role in success. This reinforced that SEO should be a priority from the start.
