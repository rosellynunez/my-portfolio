Kronaby Web Page Redesing

About

📌 Kronaby designs hybrid watches that blend cutting-edge technology with minimalist Swedish design.

Low direct traffic and sales hindered their growth. The PrestaShop platform created inefficiencies in design, development, and delivery processes.

💡 Our solution: A complete website redesign and migration to Festina's unified, Bootstrap-based digital ecosystem. This created a cohesive brand experience and streamlined design and development workflows.

See it alive

How I helped

I was responsible for the entire UX/UI process for the Kronaby website redesign.

  • 🔹 User research and competitive analysis to identify pain points.
  • 🔹 Developed a UX strategy that aligned with business goals.
  • 🔹 Designed wireframes and prototypes to test new ideas.
  • 🔹 Worked closely with strategy, marketing, and development teams to ensure a smooth implementation.

1. Research & Key Insights

Kicking off this project, I knew the Kronaby website needed a refresh and a strategic overhaul to drive business results. It also had to fit within Festina's existing digital ecosystem (which meant some design constraints, especially for product grids and pages). My focus was on improving what needed it while keeping what worked. Since the old site lacked any tracking, I started by:


  • ✔ Interviewed users & stakeholders to uncover frustrations and expectations.
  • ✔ Analyzed competitor websites to benchmark best practices in e-commerce.
  • ✔ Reviewed the site's structure and content to identify inconsistencies.

2. High-level problems

After my research, I identified these key issues:

  • ❌ Difficult navigation.
  • ❌ Unorganized content.
  • ❌ Too much information per page.
  • ❌ Lack of knowledge about what is a hibrid watch.
  • ❌ Lack of SEO strategy.
🫤

3. Hypothesis and Findings

We hypothesized that:

  • ➡️ Making navigation easier and clearly explaining hybrid watch features would get more people interested and lead to more sales.
  • ➡️ Implementing an SEO strategy would increase organic traffic.

To validate this, I:

  • ➡️ Talked to coworkers and some users to understand how they browsed the page (it was like a guerrilla validation because we had to do it fast, right before they started developing!).
  • ➡️ Did A/B tests with different menus, using quick sketches.

Findings:

  • ✅ People didn't understand what a hybrid watch was → We added educational content to explain it better.
  • ✅ Confusing navigation was making people leave → We simplified the categories and how people moved through the page.
  • ✅ We weren't using SEO well and people weren't finding us on Google → We added well-structured content so Google could find us more easily.

4. UX Decisions

Based on our research, I made the following key UX decisions:

  • ✅ Navigation → Simplified the site structure to improve usability.
  • ✅ Content → Standardized heading styles and improved readability.
  • ✅ E-commerce Optimization → We revamped the home and landing pages to get people more engaged and convert them into customers.
👍

🟠 A strategic vision for this project also included redesigning the product grid, product page, and checkout. But, because these parts of the website were connected to other websites in the Festina Group, we needed to work on them in a separate, bigger project later on.

5. UI Decisions: Colors, Typography, and Aesthetics

Typography: Balancing Aesthetics and Performance

The Kronaby app originally used Avenir Next and Minion, but these fonts were unavailable on Google Fonts, causing performance issues. My solution was DM Sans, a modern, minimalist typeface that aligns with Kronaby's aesthetic while ensuring better readability and faster load times.

Color Palette: A Premium and Modern Identity

Kronaby's brand identity is rooted in elegance and innovation, which I translated into a refined color scheme:

  • ✔ Primary Colors: A sophisticated mint green (#417178), representing precision and technology, paired with a rich brown (#785954) that adds warmth and heritage.
  • ✔ Secondary Palette: Developed by adjusting the saturation and luminosity of the primary colors, creating depth and visual variety.
  • ✔ Semantic Colors: Used strategically to enhance usability and reinforce key actions.

Application of Colors in UI

On the product grid page, I applied the brand colors to enhance clarity and engagement:

  • ✔ Background: Mint-cool-100 provides a subtle, elegant foundation.
  • ✔ Typography & Icons: Mint-cool-900, ensuring strong contrast and readability.
  • ✔ Interactive Elements: Brown-vivid-500 as an accent for interactive icons like the “like” button, creating a visually cohesive experience.

6. Collaboration with Teams

Bringing Kronaby's brand to life online required close collaboration with multiple teams across different locations:

  • ✔ Spain (Marketing Team): Ensured the new website aligned with Festina Group's
  • ✔ Sweden (Strategic Team): Maintained consistency between the website and Kronaby's mobile app experience.

  • 🔹 Bi-weekly meetings kept us aligned and the project moving forward.
  • 🔹I acted as a bridge between design, marketing, and strategy teams, facilitating decision-making and maintaining a user-centric approach throughout the process.

7. Adherence to Bootstrap Guidelines & Scalable Components

For the Kronaby redesign, we used all the existing components to ensure consistency across Festina's ecosystem. Plus, it's fully prepared for their switch to Tailwind—like building with LEGOs, where everything fits together seamlessly. But I noticed we needed a few new pieces. So, we created a custom component to explain Kronaby's hybrid watch features. It's modular, adaptable, and completely shareable across Festina's brands.

8. Final Results

9. What about metrics?

I finished this project in my last week at Festina Group, so I didn't have time to track final metrics. However, if I had stayed, I would have measured:

  • 📊 Direct Traffic → How many users visited the site directly.
  • 📊 Conversion Rate → How many visitors completed a purchase.
  • Average Session Duration → How long users stayed on the site.
  • 📊 Bounce Rate → How quickly users left the site.

I redesigned the site to attract more visitors, increase engagement, and optimize the checkout experience. A planned checkout redesign would have further improved conversions, but according the plan, that would be part of another project.
